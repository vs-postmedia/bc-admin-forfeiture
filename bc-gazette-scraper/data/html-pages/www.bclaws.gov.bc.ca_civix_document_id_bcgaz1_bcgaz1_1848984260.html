CIVIL FORFEITURE

In the Matter of Part 3.1 (Administrative Forfeiture) of the Civil Forfeiture Act [SBC 2005, C. 29] - the CFA

NOTICE IS HEREBY GIVEN THAT:

On January 30, 2016, at the 300 block of 10th Street, New Westminster, B.C., Peace Officer(s) of the Burnaby RCMP seized, at the time indicated, the subject property, described as: $270 CAD, two Arcâ€™teryx jackets, Exp Core pants, Exp Core leggings, a Hugo Boss belt, a Reebok Edmonton Oilers Jersey, an Iphone 6, and a Motorola cell phone, on or about 20:47 Hours.

The subject property was seized because there was evidence that the subject property had been obtained by the commission of an offence (or offences) under sections 354(1)(Possession of property obtained by crime) and 322 (Theft) of the Criminal Code of Canada.

Notice is hereby given that the subject property, CFO file Number: 2017-4385, is subject to forfeiture under Part 3.1 of the CFA and will be forfeited to the Government for disposal by the Director of Civil Forfeiture unless a notice of dispute is filed with the Director within the time period set out in this notice.

A notice of dispute may be filed by a person who claims to have an interest in all or part of the subject property. The notice of dispute must be filed within 60 days of the date upon which this notice is first published.

You may obtain a notice of dispute that meets the requirements of Section 14.07 of the CFA, from: www.gov.bc.ca/civilforfeiture. The notice must be in writing, signed in the presence of a lawyer or notary public, and mailed to the Civil Forfeiture Office, PO Box 9234 Station Provincial Government, Victoria, B.C. V8W 9J1.
