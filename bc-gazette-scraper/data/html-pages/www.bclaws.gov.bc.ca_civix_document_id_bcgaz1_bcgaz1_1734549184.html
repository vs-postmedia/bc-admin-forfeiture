MINISTERIAL ORDER

Pursuant to Ministerial Order 2019 M206 and effective June 7, 2019, the Honourable Mike Farnworth, Minister of Public Safety and Solicitor General, orders that,

1. effective the date of this Order, the December 9, 1992 order of E. Robert A. Edwards, Deputy Attorney General - Designation of Qualified Technicians (1993) BC Gaz I, 146 is repealed, and

2. pursuant to the Criminal Code (Canada) section 320.4(b)(i), the following classes of persons are designated as qualified to take samples of blood (“qualified technicians”) for the purposes of Part VIII. I of the Criminal Code (Canada):

a. A registrant of the British Columbia College of Nursing Professionals who is authorized to use the title:

i. “registered nurse;”

ii. “nurse practitioner,” or

iii. “registered psychiatric nurse”

b. A person working in a hospital who, in the normal course of their duties, takes blood samples

The full order is available on BC Laws.[no7]

MINISTERIAL ORDER

Pursuant to Ministerial Order 2019 M228 and effective June 27, 2019, the Honourable Mike Farnworth, Minister of Public Safety and Solicitor General, orders that,

1. pursuant to the Criminal Code (Canada) section 320.4(b)(ii), the forensic specialists and forensic technologists employed by the Royal Canadian Mounted Police Toxicology Services are designated as a class of persons that is qualified, for the purposes of Part VIII.I of the Criminal Code (Canada), to analyze samples of bodily substances;

and

2. pursuant to the Criminal Code (Canada) section 320.4(c) the forensic specialists and forensic technologists employed by the Royal Canadian Mounted Police Toxicology Services are designated as a class of persons that is qualified, for the purposes of Part VIII.I of the Criminal Code (Canada), to certify that an alcohol standard is suitable for use with an approved instrument.

The full order is available on BC Laws. [no7]
