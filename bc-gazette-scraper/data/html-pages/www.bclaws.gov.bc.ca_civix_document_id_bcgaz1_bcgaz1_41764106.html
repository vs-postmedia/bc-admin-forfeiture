CIVIL FORFEITURE

In the Matter of Part 3.1 (Administrative Forfeiture) of the Civil Forfeiture Act [SBC 2005, C. 29] - the CFA

NOTICE IS HEREBY GIVEN THAT:

The property described below is subject to forfeiture under Part 3.1 of the CFA and will be forfeited to the Government for disposal by the Director of Civil Forfeiture unless a notice of dispute is filed with the Director within the time period set out in this notice.

A notice of dispute may be filed by a person who claims to have an interest in all or part of the subject property. The notice of dispute must be filed within 60 days of the date upon which this notice is first published. You may obtain a notice of dispute that meets the requirements of Section 14.07 of the CFA, from: www.gov.bc.ca/civilforfeiture.

At the 5800 block of Vedder Road, and at the 45600 block of Alma Avenue, Chilliwack, B.C., Peace Officer(s) of the Chilliwack RCMP seized, on the date indicated, the subject property, described as:

- $315.25 CAD on 2020-6-4 (Vedder Road)

- $67,135 CAD on 2020-6-4 (Alma Avenue)

- $4,180 CAD on 2020-6-4 (Alma Avenue)

CFO file Number: R-08555; AC-10524.

The subject property ($315.25 CAD) was seized because there was evidence that the subject property had been obtained by the commission of an offence (or offences) under section 5(2) (Possess Trafficking) Controlled Drugs and Substances Act of Canada (CDSA). The subject property ($67,135 CAD and $4,180 CAD) was seized because there was a warrant authorized by the court to search for evidence of an offence (or offences) under section 5(2) CDSA.
