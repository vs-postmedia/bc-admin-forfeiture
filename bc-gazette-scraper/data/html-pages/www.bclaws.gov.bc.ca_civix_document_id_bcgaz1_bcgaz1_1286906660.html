A notice of dispute may be filed by a person who claims to have an interest in all or part of the subject property. The notice of dispute must be filed within 60 days of the date upon which this notice is first published. No. S171383

Victoria Registry

In the Supreme Court of British Columbia

Civil Forfeiture Action in Rem Against

$1,996 in Canadian currency seized from Jolina Madeleine Rothrock by the Kelowna RCMP on December 20, 2016 (the “Money”) and its proceeds

Between

Director of Civil Forfeiture

Plaintiff

And

The Owners and all Others Interested in the Money, in Particular, Jolina Madeleine Rothrock and Luke Anthony Ploegmakers

Defendants

ADVERTISEMENT

To: the defendants, Jolina Madeleine Rothrock and Luke Anthony Ploegmakers

TAKE NOTICE THAT on 26/OCT/2017 an order was made for service on you of a notice of civil claim issued from the Victoria Registry of the Supreme Court of British Columbia in proceeding number 17 1383 by way of this advertisement.

In the proceeding, the plaintiff, Director of Civil Forfeiture, claims the following relief against you: forfeiture of the $1,996 in Canadian currency seized from Jolina Madeleine Rothrock by the Kelowna RCMP on December 20, 2016 (the “Money”) and its proceeds.

You must file a responding pleading within the period required under the Supreme Court Civil Rules failing which further proceedings, including judgment, may be taken against you without notice to you.

You may obtain, from the Victoria Registry, at 850 Burdett Avenue, Victoria, BC, a copy of the notice of civil claim and the order providing for service by this advertisement.

This advertisement is placed by the plaintiff, Director of Civil Forfeiture, whose address for service is 1001 Douglas Street, Victoria, BC, V8W 9J7, via fax (250) 387-4002. [no9]
