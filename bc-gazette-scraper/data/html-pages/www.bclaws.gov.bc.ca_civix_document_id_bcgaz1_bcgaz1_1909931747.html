CIVIL FORFEITURE

PUBLIC NOTICE

To all persons who may have been involved in the following:

You may be entitled to share in moneys recovered by the Director of Civil Forfeiture arising from the case of Director v. $45,000 and $6,522 CAD.

We cannot pay you any interest or gains on funds that were lost. Compensation is limited to your actual loss and does not cover pain or suffering. Also, if you already received compensation in relation to this incident, you must tell us about that in your application.

To make a claim, you, or someone who acts for you, must submit an application form and copies of all documentation pertaining to the loss.

You may send the application form and documents to us by mail, courier, fax or e-mail. Our contact information and instructions for obtaining the application form are located at the bottom of this ad. Please be aware that we cannot consider claims received after the close of business on May 31, 2018.

If you need an application, we will send you one. Call us toll free at 1-844-356-1560, e-mail us at civilfo@gov.bc.ca, or write us at PO Box 9234 Stn Prov Govt, Victoria, BC V8W 9J1. [mh8]

CIVIL FORFEITURE

In the Matter of Part 3.1 (Administrative Forfeiture) of the Civil Forfeiture Act [SBC 2005, C. 29] - the CFA

NOTICE IS HEREBY GIVEN THAT:

The property described below is subject to forfeiture under Part 3.1 of the CFA and will be forfeited to the Government for disposal by the Director of Civil Forfeiture unless a notice of dispute is filed with the Director within the time period set out in this notice.

A notice of dispute may be filed by a person who claims to have an interest in all or part of the subject property. The notice of dispute must be filed within 60 days of the date upon which this notice is first published. You may obtain a notice of dispute that meets the requirements of Section 14.07 of the CFA, from: www.gov.bc.ca/civilforfeiture.
